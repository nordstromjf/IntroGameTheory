Now we want to know what Player 1's payoff will be as she varies the probability, \(p\text{,}\) with which she plays B. We can draw a graph where the \(x\)-axis represents to probability with which she plays B (\(p\)) and the \(y\)-axis represents the expected payoff (\(m\)). See Figure 3.2.3.

Thus, when Player 1 plays only A, she is playing B with probability 0; when Player 1 plays only B, she is playing B with probability 1. It might be easier to remember if you label your graph as in Figure 3.2.3.
