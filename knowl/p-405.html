Use substitution to find the point of intersection.

\begin{align*} 2x \amp = \amp -2x+1\\ 4x \amp = \amp 1\\ x \amp = \amp {1\over 4} \end{align*}

Substituting \(x={1\over 4}\) back in to either original equation, say \(y=2x\text{,}\) gives us \(y={1\over 2}\text{.}\) Thus, the point of intersection is \((1/4, 1/2)\text{.}\)
